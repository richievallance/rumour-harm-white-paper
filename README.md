## Abstract

This white paper examines rumour propagation, reputational damage, and institutional communication failure through a sociological and digital-sociology lens. It addresses the mechanisms by which unverified claims enter and persist in public narrative, the failure modes of institutional response, and evidence control in high-stakes reputational contexts. This is constitutionally separate from Leonardo / Da Valenca research and from the Universal Simulation Construct framework.

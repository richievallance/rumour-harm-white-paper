## Abstract

This paper argues that Codex Madrid I is best understood not as a miscellaneous notebook of mechanical sketches, but as a structured corpus of reusable engineering modules whose coherence emerges when the manuscript is read simultaneously at three levels: text as demonstration, reverse folio sequence as instruction, and marginal note as governing law.

Six original priority claims are registered:

- **PC-1** — Complete flintlock prototype (c.1493–97), 115 years before le Bourgeoys
- **PC-2** — Resonant impulse accumulation as the theoretical basis of escapement physics
- **PC-3** — Combined clock-and-orrery modular platform identification
- **PC-4** — Chiaravalle abbey clock as precision dating anchor
- **PC-5** — Seepage alignment as intentional design registration
- **PC-6** — Over-centre toggle-lock doctrine

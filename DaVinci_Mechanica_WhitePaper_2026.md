# **CHAPTER I**

## **The Nature of the Codex: How Madrid I Must Be Read**

### **1.1  The Standard Difficulty — and Why It Is Misplaced**

The standard difficulty in reading Leonardo's mechanical manuscripts is that they appear to oscillate between brilliance and incompletion. A page can contain a complete mechanism, a partial variant, an unexplained diagram, a note of extraordinary theoretical density, and an apparently unrelated vocabulary list. This has encouraged generations of readers to treat the notebooks as a workshop residue: invaluable but fragmentary, suggestive but not architecturally ordered.

This paper argues that this is the wrong scale of reading. The codices are indeed fragmentary in one sense — they distribute functions across multiple leaves — but not in the sense usually assumed. They are incomplete as assembled machines, not as engineering language. Their true coherence lies in the recurrence of modules, laws, and transformations across pages. A spring is never merely a spring: it belongs to a doctrine of force diminution and compensation. A gear is never merely a gear: it belongs to a theory of ratio, direction, wear, and contact. A bell does not merely strike: it closes a release cascade already prepared by lever logic, clutch logic, and timing logic.

Once that interpretive shift is made, the codex begins to behave less like a cabinet of curiosities and more like an engineering treatise dispersed across folios — which is precisely what Leonardo's own internal citation apparatus confirms it to be.

### **1.2  The Three-Layer Reading Model**

The codex becomes fully coherent only when read across three simultaneous modes.

**Mode 1 — Text as Demonstration.**

The main body of each folio, read forward, shows what a mechanism does. It gives behaviour, relation, consequence, and comparison. When Leonardo discusses gear ratios, spring return, or water percussion, he is demonstrating the behaviour of a completed system. The forward direction corresponds to visible outcome.

**Mode 2 — Reverse Sequence as Instruction.**

The deeper insight — and the one central to this project — is that the codex also reads backward as a dependency system. Later folios show visible outcomes whose prerequisites are defined earlier. If one begins at a bell output and reads backward: the release system precedes it; the regulator precedes that; the force store precedes that; the energy input precedes that; and in the margins, the laws that make the chain valid appear. This is not mystical reversal. It is mechanical dependency. Outputs must be traced backward into their causes.

This reverse dependency model explains why some folios appear 'ahead' of their explanation. They are not ahead; they are terminal stages. They show the accomplished act. Earlier folios provide the enabling conditions. Forward reading demonstrates. Backward reading instructs.

**Mode 3 — Margins as Law.**

The margins are the most neglected stratum. Yet they often contain the most compressed and most universal rules. The famous water-raising marginal note is the clearest example — it is not an ornament but a governing law: external energy must be introduced, stored, and then released. That single note disqualifies perpetual motion and establishes the operating principle of the entire platform. Sidenotes on material hardness, noise, load, and geometry are not commentary. They are constraints. In modern engineering language, they are design rules.

**The central methodological formula:**

*Text = demonstration of systems. Reverse sequence = construction instruction. Margins = governing laws. Without all three, the codex fragments. With all three, it resolves.*

The second: 'Tadeo, figliolo di Nicholaio del Turcho, ebbe finiti anni 9 la vigilia di Sa' Michele, a dì 28 di settembre 1497' — 28 September 1497, the Lute Player page. # **CHAPTER II**

## **The Energy Doctrine: Why Madrid I Opens with the Rejection of Perpetual Motion**

### **2.1  Not a Philosophical Preface — a Boundary Condition**

Leonardo's opening rejection of perpetual motion is the first engineering boundary condition of the manuscript, not a philosophical preface. His language is sharper than mere scepticism. In the primary text of BNE 5869:

*'**Io ho trovato infra le altre superchie le inpossibili credulità degli omini la ciercha del moto chontinuo... Ora io intendo fare questa limosina a questa setta d**'**investigatori, cioè, di dare loro tanto di quiete in tale ciercha quanto durerà questa mia pichola opera.**'** — I have found among the other superfluous and impossible credulities of men the search for continuous motion... Now I intend to make this alms to this sect of investigators, that is, to give them so much rest in such search as will last the duration of this small work of mine. (BNE 5869, RSV translation 2026.)*

The contemptuous term — 'questa setta d'investigatori' (this sect of investigators) — signals that perpetual motion is not a live option. It is a category error. The rebuke elsewhere in the same folio is still sharper: 'O s[c]enpi, overamente [omi]ni sanza naturale, [che] volete voi?' — 'O simpletons, or truly men without nature, what do you want?' This is the language of a scientist dismissing superstition, not the language of a philosopher considering possibilities.

### **2.2  The Marginal Water Note — The Platform in Miniature**

The most consequential text in the entire opening sequence is not in the main body but in the left margin of the same folio. This has been consistently overlooked in the scholarship. The margin reads:

*'**Cioè, levare con i strumenti l**'**acqua qu[i]eta, come di padule, pozo o di mare, e cqu[e]sta acqua levata **'**nn suo discicendere usi a ffare come ffa l**'**altra acqua alle mulina. O s[c]enpi...**'** — That is, to raise with instruments still water, as from marsh, well or sea, and this water raised in its descent is made to do as the other water does at the mills. O simpletons... (BNE 5869 marginal note, RSV translation 2026.)*

This marginal note is the entire modular platform stated in one sentence: external energy input (labour or mechanical device raising water) → storage (elevated water = potential energy) → controlled release (water falling drives mill). The 'perpetual motion' fools are trying to make the mill run the pump that fills the reservoir. Leonardo has already solved this: bring energy from outside, store it, release it. Input-agnostic platform. Stored energy. Regulated release.

Everything in the subsequent 190 folios is a specification of how to implement this three-part sequence.

### **2.3  The Resonant Impulse Analysis — Bounded Accumulation**

The resonant impulse passage (BNE 5911) strengthens the energy doctrine with its most sophisticated physical argument. The full primary text, now confirmed from the transcript:

*'**Io ho inparato dai bochalari chome l**'**inpeto de**'** lor torni riserva in sé alquanto la natura di quel moto che da essi bochalari è dato loro... Quando tu trovi la mola ferma, e ttu le dia 3 volte e poi la lassci, essa seguita poi un moto d**'**una cierta velocità. Ma sse tti ritorni... tu le darai assai magior moto che prima non le desti... E così senpre, insino a ccierto tenpo, s**'**aumenterà la velocità.**'** — I have learned from potters how the impetus of their wheels retains in itself somewhat the nature of the motion given them... When you find the millstone still and give it 3 turns and let it go, it continues with a certain velocity... if you return to give it another 3 turns you will give it much greater motion than you first gave... And thus always, until a certain time, velocity will increase. (BNE 5911, RSV translation 2026.)*

The limiting phrase 'insino a ccierto tenpo' — 'until a certain time' — is decisive. Leonardo is not flirting with perpetual motion. He is describing bounded accumulation: motion already present in the system contributes to later motion, but it does not abolish the need for input. The system remembers impulse temporarily. It does not generate energy.

This passage also identifies the source of the resonant accumulation law: potters' wheels observed in craft practice. Leonardo learned this physical principle from watching artisans work. This is his epistemological method: observe, measure, abstract the law. The bochalari (potters) are his empirical witness for impulse accumulation just as Giulio is his empirical witness for bearing wear.

# **CHAPTER III**

## **Storage, Compensation, and the Governor Principle**

### **3.1  Why Spring Storage Is the Core Problem**

Once energy is admitted as necessary, the next question is storage. Madrid I repeatedly returns to spring systems, screw systems, and compensatory geometries because Leonardo is solving one engineering problem: force is rarely constant at the moment it is needed. It must be accumulated and delivered in a controlled way.

The spring case (chassa della molla) is therefore not a side device but a key structural layer of the platform. A spring stores energy compactly. But Leonardo immediately identifies the problem: spring force diminishes across the discharge cycle. A raw spring does not deliver constant power. Hence the critical importance of the pyramidal screw and the family of vite perpetua devices.

### **3.2  The Governor Principle — Mirror Diminution**

Leonardo states the governor principle with maximum theoretical precision in the De forza e peso section (BNE 5876):

*'**Tutte le molle non usano fare equal ofitio in ciasscuna loro meza volta... El simile fano quelle chon corda, che ss**'**avolta sulla vite piramidale, che tanta molla consuma la volta più sottole della vite piramidale, quanto la volta più grossa.**'** — All springs do not produce equal effect in each of their half-turns... The same do those with cord, that wind on the pyramidal screw, that a spring consumes the thinner turn of the pyramidal screw as much as the thicker turn. (BNE 5876, RSV translation 2026.)*

The pyramidal screw (fusee equivalent) compensates for spring force decay by continuously varying the effective radius of the drive drum. As the spring weakens, the drum's working radius increases proportionally, maintaining constant torque at the output shaft. This is the heart of every mechanical clock built for the next four centuries, stated here with complete theoretical clarity in the 1490s.

The governor principle can be stated: the resistance mechanism must mirror the force diminution curve of the spring. What the spring loses, the fusee compensates. Beginning strong together, ending weak together — so that throughout the entire discharge cycle the product of force and radius (the torque) remains constant.

### **3.3  The Dead-Point Doctrine — Geometric Force Loss**

One of the most sophisticated mechanical insights in the entire transcript sequence appears in BNE 5955, the spring-return geometry folio:

*'**hessa non potrà perché la contralieva b a che **'**n prima chondusse f dove tu llo vedi, ancora lei fa moto circulare intorno al suo polo, onde quando poi la molla vole rimettere f al suo primo sito, ella ha la linia b a che lle contradicie perché l**'**angolo b a f hè ottuso sopra il retto. Cioè, che prima fu retto, poi passò hesso grado di retto, e ffeciosi ottuso.**'** — It cannot return because the counter-lever b a that first conducted f to where you see it still makes circular motion around its pole, so that when the spring wishes to return f to its original position, it has the line b a which contradicts it because the angle b a f is obtuse above the right angle. That is, that first it was right [90°], then it passed through that degree of right, and made itself obtuse. (BNE 5955, RSV translation 2026.)*

Leonardo identifies the precise geometric condition under which a spring-loaded mechanism cannot return to its original position: once the angle of the driven linkage passes through 90° (the toggle point) and becomes obtuse, the spring's force vector is now directed away from the return path. The mechanism is over-centre — locked in place, with the spring unable to return it.

The solution he prescribes: two separate poles (pivot points) replace the single-pole arrangement. With two poles, the linkage geometry can be arranged so that the operative angle never crosses 90° during the working stroke. The dead-point is avoided by design.

This is the over-centre toggle-lock principle — the same principle governing toggle clamps, locking pliers, and the human knee joint. Its systematic identification as a design failure mode has no known precedent in the Renaissance mechanical literature.

### **3.4  Spring Manufacturing Protocol — Complete Fabrication Specification**

BNE 5898 provides the most technically dense document in the transcript set: a complete spring manufacturing protocol for clock mainsprings. The specification proceeds:

1. Cut steel rod to form n b. 2. Mount in the draw-plate instrument, clamp with screw m, draw through the die. 3. Reheat and redraw through a progressively narrower die. Repeat annealing and redrawing until the strip reaches the thickness of a large sheet of paper. 4. Coil and place in the spring case. 5. Temper by annealing to azure/blue colour in molten lead at glass-working furnace temperature, achieving uniform through-thickness temperature.

The blue azure colour is the visual indicator of the correct tempering temperature (approximately 290–300°C for steel). The molten lead bath achieves uniform temperature throughout the spring's cross-section — superior to air heating. This is precision metallurgical temperature control in the 1490s.

The same folio contains two further structural engineering principles. First, the screw tension doctrine: 'Tutte le forze che ttu fai fare alle viti, fa che piutossto tirino ch'elle spinghino' — all forces you make screws do, make them pull rather than push. Under tension screws straighten and align; under compression they buckle. This is Euler's column buckling principle stated 250 years before Euler. Second, the synchronous multi-screw doctrine: all screws in an assembly must turn simultaneously at equal rates, because the one that overtightens bears excess load and breaks.

### **3.5  The Maesstra — Personal Calibration Instrument**

Within the Batch 2 sequence Leonardo describes a calibration instrument he built for himself — the Maesstra — which he uses to verify the compensation ratio between spring force decay and fusee profile. He refers to it in the feminine as 'she.' This is both a technical instrument and a personal memory marker.

The Maesstra confirms that Madrid I is not theoretical abstraction but a record of actual workshop practice. The pyramidal screw specification was physically measured, tested, and refined. The Maesstra is the evidence that Leonardo was a practising engineer, not merely a theorist.

# **CHAPTER IV**

## **Regulation: Escapement, Ratchet, and Controlled Release**

### **4.1  The Problem of Regulation**

If storage solves the problem of retaining energy, regulation solves the problem of not releasing it all at once. Here Madrid I is astonishingly mature. The escapement material, the one-way clutch logic, the ratchet doctrine, and the chiave cascade together form a complete philosophy of controlled release that has no rival in the contemporary mechanical literature.

### **4.2  The Complete Verge-and-Foliot Escapement**

BNE 5893 provides the complete specification of the verge-and-foliot escapement, with the complete action sequence in primary Italian:

*'**La rota dentata n entra sopra il polo b m. He ogni ora la lieva k è tirata una volta inanzi, e llo sscarpello e si move per contrario moto, e sspinge tutto il dente t. Poi esso scarpello torna indirieto per forza della molla s, e chade sul dente o, e quivi si ferma insino all**'**altra hora.**'** — The toothed wheel n enters on pole b m. And every hour the lever k is pulled once forward, and the pallet e moves in contrary motion, and pushes the entire tooth t. Then the pallet returns backward by force of spring s, and falls on tooth o, and there stops until the next hour. (BNE 5893, RSV translation 2026.)*

'Questo moto è molto variabile di fatica. E cquel dela rota mai si muta.' — This motion is very variable in effort. And the wheel's motion never changes. (BNE 5893.)

This last sentence is the philosophical statement of what the escapement achieves: variable effort in, constant rotation out. The pallet varies enormously in force across push, return, contact, and release — but the wheel's motion never changes. The escapement absorbs force variation and delivers regularity. This is the essential function of all regulation mechanisms.

Two further findings from BNE 5893 not previously registered: the Mobile/Immobile state diagram (the first explicit two-state kinematic diagram in the codex — escapement shown in active and resting states), and the resting-tooth confirmation: 'si ferma insino all'altra hora' (stops there until the next hour). This is a one-per-hour striking clock, not a continuous tick-tock oscillator. The escapement rests on a single tooth between hourly strikes.

### **4.3  The Chiaravalle Reference — Dating Anchor**

The most important geographical and chronological anchor in the entire codex appears in BNE 5892:

*'**Poli in poli, chome in Chiaravalle.**'** — Poles within poles, as in Chiaravalle. (BNE 5892.)*

Chiaravalle is the Cistercian abbey founded in 1135, six kilometres south of Milan, housing one of the oldest mechanical tower clocks in northern Italy. Leonardo had visited it. He studied its nested pole-and-axle gear train and encoded it as the known reference example for the poli in poli system he was documenting. The Chiaravalle clock is still standing. Its gear train can be physically compared to Leonardo's diagrams. This is Milan. This is the Sforza period. This is approximately 1492–1497.

The same BNE 5892 folio provides a further original finding: the tooth self-centring mechanism. After tooth m releases from the ratchet, the next tooth g automatically centres itself to the pole axis. Leonardo annotates: 'Qui i' non fo disegno se non del moto che ssegue il mobile usscito del contatto del suo motore' — Here I draw nothing except the motion that follows the moving element once it has left contact with its mover. This is a conscious kinematic diagram annotation: Leonardo is explicitly drawing the motion of disengagement, not the form of the mechanism. He is practising kinematics as a discipline distinct from statics.

### **4.4  The De Rocha e Rrota — The Complete Transmission Law**

BNE 5900 provides three gear laws in primary Italian that together constitute the complete transmission doctrine:

*'**Se lla rocha de**'** movere la rota, li sspati de**'** denti d**'**essa rocha debbono essere magiori che quelli della rota. He sse la rota avessi a movere la rocha, fa per l**'**oposito detto.**'** — If the ratchet is to move the wheel, the tooth spacings of the ratchet must be larger than those of the wheel. And if the wheel is to move the ratchet, do the opposite. (BNE 5900.)*

*'**Se vorai che lla rota del primo moto abbi una rota che volti per lo verso che volti chome hessa, hè neciessario che sia nel 3° grado di moto.**'** — If you want the wheel of the first motion to have a wheel that turns in the same direction as itself, it must be in the 3rd degree of motion. (BNE 5900.)*

*'**se si tocheranno di fori, esse si volteranno in contrario l**'**una all**'**altra. Ma sse ll**'**una col suo di fori volterà l**'**altra di dentro, esse si volteranno per un medesimo verso**'** — if they touch on their outsides they will turn in contrary directions. But if one with its outside turns the other on the inside, they will turn in the same direction. (BNE 5900.)*

Three laws confirmed in primary source: tooth spacing reciprocity law (ratchet drives wheel: wider spacing required; wheel drives ratchet: narrower); the third-degree same-direction law (naming the odd/even reversal law as 'third degree of motion'); and the internal-vs-external engagement law (topological determination of output direction).

### **4.5  The Ratchet Doctrine — Spring-Loaded Pawl Specification**

BNE 5909 provides the complete fabrication specification for the spring-loaded pawl — the most precisely specified named mechanical component in the transcript set:

*'**a hè uno pezo d**'**acciaro ben tenperato he non rinvenuto. Il quale acciaro è messo in poli a uso d**'**usscio, e ha una molla di dentro che senpre lo sspingie in fori, adosso a**'** denti dello strumento che llo inchassa.**'** — a is a piece of well-tempered and un-softened steel. This steel is placed in poles like a door-hinge, and has an internal spring that always pushes it outward against the teeth of the mechanism that houses it. (BNE 5909.)*

Material: tempered but not re-annealed steel (hard without brittleness). Form: pivot-mounted like a door-hinge. Function: spring-loaded outward at all times. The bidirectional behaviour is explicitly stated: forward driven by wheel teeth; pole free to rotate backward. This is the complete one-way ratchet specification. The same folio gives the 10:1 quantitative wear law: 80-tooth wheel with 8-tooth ratchet produces 10× wear acceleration on the ratchet (because the ratchet turns 10 times for every one wheel revolution).

# **CHAPTER V**

## **Transmission, Structure, and the Bearing Architecture**

### **5.1  The Equality Line Doctrine**

The most fundamental principle of gear theory in the codex is the equality line: the geometric boundary of effective force application at the tooth contact face. Force applied within the equality line (toward the gear's centre) transmits as useful rotational torque. Force applied outside generates friction, deformation, and heat. This doctrine governs every gear design in the codex and appears, in different forms, at every scale of the treatise.

The equality line doctrine does not apply only to gears. It is Leonardo's universal principle of force transmission: any mechanical contact must be analysed in terms of the force vector at the contact point relative to the equality boundary. The same analysis applies to bearings (pole in hole), screws (thread contact), and chains (link contact).

### **5.2  The Odd/Even Reversal Law**

The direction control rule for the entire transmission architecture: an odd number of meshed gears in series produces output rotation in the same direction as input; an even number produces reversed output. Named in BNE 5900 as the 'third degree of motion' for same-direction output. Its implications govern the design of every output branch of the unified platform: the orrery requires simultaneous same-direction and reverse outputs; the clock requires specific rotation direction for the cascade; the retrograde planetary motion requires periodic reversal. All solvable before a single gear is drawn.

### **5.3  The Semicircle Direction Law**

BNE 5910 provides the most consequential transmission finding of the Batch 5 transcripts:

*'**Tutte le roche che ssi possan voltare da denti d**'**una medesima rota, si volteranno in contrario moto d**'**essa rota. E anchora quelle tali roche che ssi troveranno tochare l**'**un semicirchulo della rota che lle move, si volteran per chontrario moto a cquel che ffano l**'**altre roche, mosse dall**'**altro semicirchulo.**'** — All the ratchets that can be turned by the teeth of the same wheel will turn in contrary motion to that wheel. And also those ratchets that will be found touching one semicircle of the wheel that moves them will turn in contrary motion to what the other ratchets do, moved by the other semicircle. (BNE 5910.)*

This is the semicircle contact-geometry law. A single driving wheel can produce simultaneous opposite-direction outputs by distributing driven elements across both semicircles. This is the physical mechanism behind the orrery's retrograde module: one driving wheel, two semicircles, two simultaneous directions. The same principle with smaller scale is the ball bearing's function: balls distributed across the full circumference experience the same contact geometry, producing uniform load distribution.

### **5.4  The Ball Bearing — Rolling Element Anti-Friction System**

BNE 5910 Figura 1 provides the complete ball bearing specification:

*'**a b c d e f g h son palle di legnio **'**n iscanbio di curri per movere un peso. i k l m n l p q son rote inpolate, che tengano dette palle in regola, che fanno l**'**ofitio del voltare e non posson fugire.**'** — a b c d e f g h are wooden balls in exchange for runners to move a weight. i k l m n l p q are pivoted wheels that keep these balls in order, that perform the function of rotation and cannot escape. (BNE 5910.)*

The design: wooden balls constrained between two concentric rings by pole-wheels that maintain equal spacing. Rolling replaces sliding; friction is reduced to point contact. ### **5.5  Bearing Durability — The Wear Laws**

BNE 5895 and BNE 5894 together provide the complete bearing durability doctrine:

Load distribution law (BNE 5895): 'When the weight is great or truly fast in motion, then the poles must be long, so that the weight distributes itself across many contact points.' Long poles spread load over more contact length, reducing unit pressure and wear rate.

Speed law (BNE 5895): Fast-moving poles wear at the same rate as heavy-load poles. Speed and load are mechanically equivalent in their wear effect. This is a proto-statement of the Archard wear equation.

Hard-on-hard doctrine (BNE 5895): 'It is necessary to make hard with hard. So you will make the poles of the wheels of tempered steel, and similarly the holes that receive them will also be of tempered steel.' Matching hardness produces equal mutual wear — both surfaces diminish predictably and uniformly.

Quantitative wear law (BNE 5909): 80-tooth wheel with 8-tooth ratchet produces 10× wear rate differential in favour of the wheel. The driving element always outlasts the smaller driven element in a high-ratio interaction.

### **5.6  Structural Laws — Anti-Rotation and Load Management**

BNE 5887 provides the anti-rotation cord constraint, the most structurally fundamental finding of the Batch 5 set:

*'**Le corde a b c d debono essere ferri che stieno uniti chollo sstrumenti di sopra, se none il manicho, che volta, volterebbe ancora alquanto lo strumento sottosopra.**'** — The cords a b c d must be iron rods that stay united with the instruments above, otherwise the handle, when turned, would also turn the instrument upside down. (BNE 5887.)*

When a handle applies torque to a shaft, Newton's Third Law means the housing wants to spin in the opposite direction. Iron rods connecting the housing to a fixed reference absorb this reaction torque. Without this constraint, turning the handle spins the entire device rather than the shaft. This is the structural precondition for all shaft-driven machinery to function. In platform terms: this is why the sensale needs a frame. The frame's function is defined explicitly here for the first time in the transcript sequence.

The tanaglia self-loading grip (BNE 5913): 'Quanto magiore peso fia quello che charichèrà questa tanaglia, meglio e più forte sarà sostenuto' — the greater the weight loading the tongs, the better and more strongly it is held. This is the over-centre toggle applied positively: geometric self-tightening under load. Where BNE 5955 shows the toggle as a failure mode (prevents return), BNE 5913 shows it as a design feature (increases grip). One geometric principle, two applications.

### **6.3  The Two Vite Perpetua — Complete Primary Source**

BNE 5906 provides the clearest mechanical distinction in the entire transcript set — the explicit specification of two different versions of the perpetual screw, each designed for a different application:

*'**Vite perpetua. Questa vite non è d**'**equal forza, perché più debolmente move lontano dal suo ciento che da presso quel braccio della rota da llei mosso. *'**Vite perpetua. He perché questa tal vite ha senpre quatro denti della rota che hella move in varie distantie dal suo ciento, essa adopera con chontinua equalità di forza.**'** — Perpetual screw, Version 2. Version 1 (for clocks): variable force, acceptable for clock timing where the escapement compensates. The four-tooth constant-force solution is mathematically equivalent to a fusee but achieved through tooth geometry alone, without a variable-radius drum.
